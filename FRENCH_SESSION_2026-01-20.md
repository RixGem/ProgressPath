### 1. Reflexive Verbs (Verbes Pronominaux)

Focused on reflexive verbs with specific emphasis on:

#### New Vocabulary Learned

- **se perdre** - to get lost
  - *Je me perds* (I get lost)
  - *Tu te perds* (You get lost)
  - *Il/Elle se perd* (He/She gets lost)

- **se garer** - to park (oneself/vehicle)
  - *Je me gare* (I park)
  - *Nous nous garons* (We park)
  - *Ils se garent* (They park)

#### Key Grammar Points

**Reflexive Pronoun Placement:**
- Subject pronoun + reflexive pronoun + verb
- Examples:
  - Je **me** perds souvent en ville (I often get lost in the city)
  - Elle **se** gare devant la maison (She parks in front of the house)

**Conjugation Pattern:**

### 2. Negative Imperatives (L'impÃ©ratif nÃ©gatif)

Practiced giving negative commands using reflexive verbs.

#### Practice Sentences

1. **Ne te perds pas !** 
   - Don't get lost! (informal singular)
   
2. **Ne vous perdez pas !**
   - Don't get lost! (formal/plural)
   
3. **Ne te gare pas ici !**
   - Don't park here! (informal singular)
   
4. **Ne nous garons pas trop loin !**
   - Let's not park too far! (first person plural)

#### Grammar Structure

**Formation:**
- Ne + reflexive pronoun + verb (imperative form) + pas
- The reflexive pronoun comes BEFORE the verb in negative imperatives
- This differs from affirmative imperatives where the pronoun comes after

**Comparison:**
- Affirmative: **Gare-toi ici** (Park here)
- Negative: **Ne te gare pas ici** (Don't park here)
